# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

九桥同步 sales and technical staff presenting to enterprise client IT directors, procurement leads, and architecture decision-makers at financial institutions (e.g., 金融街证券). The context is a formal meeting room, often with a projector or large display. The audience is technically sophisticated, skeptical, and time-constrained. They are evaluating whether FZS is credible, capable, and safe to adopt for mission-critical financial data infrastructure.

## Product Purpose

A Slidev-based product pitch deck introducing FZS 数据同步平台 to enterprise financial clients. Its purpose is to convey technical depth, production reliability, and domestic-database breadth in a format that commands respect without requiring a follow-up explanation. Success looks like: the audience leaves with a clear mental model of what FZS does, why it is trustworthy, and a concrete next step (POC or further discussion).

## Brand Personality

Precise · Professional · Trustworthy

Voice and tone: confident and direct, like a senior engineer who knows the system inside out. No filler language, no marketing hyperbole. Every claim is backed by specificity (actual database names, actual latency numbers, actual component names).

## Anti-references

- **No SaaS-startup aesthetics**: no decorative gradients, no rounded pill buttons, no pastel background sections, no "product hunt" energy. This is financial infrastructure, not a consumer app.
- **No dry government-report aesthetics**: no black-on-white walls of text with zero visual hierarchy. The slides must be scannable and visually structured.
- **No generic Western enterprise look**: avoid the IBM/SAP navy-and-gray grid that communicates "big but boring." FZS is a modern, technically sharp domestic platform.

## Design Principles

1. **Technical credibility over decoration**: Every design choice should reinforce trust in a complex system. Specificity, precision, and clarity are more persuasive than visual flair.
2. **Hierarchy that works at projection distance**: Enterprise decision-makers in meeting rooms need to absorb key points at a glance. Visual hierarchy must be unambiguous and readable on a projected screen.
3. **Density with dignity**: Many slides carry technical tables, component lists, and database matrices. Layout must handle dense content without looking cramped or chaotic.
4. **Chinese-first typesetting**: The primary script is Simplified Chinese. Typography decisions must treat Chinese glyphs as first-class, not as an afterthought to Latin type choices.
5. **Show competence, earn confidence**: The slides speak to sophisticated buyers evaluating mission-critical infrastructure. The design should feel like the product: engineered, not styled.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG requirements stated, but slides must be clearly legible on projectors and large displays — adequate contrast, no text below ~14px effective size on slide, no color-only information encoding.
